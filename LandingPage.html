Why TransitOps

Spreadsheets don't scale with a fleet.

Most operators still run dispatch and compliance out of shared sheets and paper logbooks. It works — until it doesn't.

Without TransitOps
  • Double-booked vehicles and scheduling conflicts discovered after dispatch
  • Vehicles sitting idle while others are overworked, with no visibility to rebalance
  • Missed maintenance windows that turn into roadside breakdowns
  • Drivers dispatched with expired licenses, caught only during an audit
  • Fuel and toll receipts entered weeks late, with inaccurate cost tracking
With TransitOps
  • Vehicles and drivers locked automatically the moment a trip is dispatched
  • Live fleet utilization on one dashboard, filterable by type, status, region
  • Maintenance logs that pull a vehicle from dispatch the second it's opened
  • License expiry enforced at the dropdown level — no eligible-driver mistakes
  • Fuel, tolls, and maintenance rolled into real-time operational cost per vehicle

Getting started

From empty fleet to first trip, in minutes.

The exact flow a new operator runs on day one.

1
Register a vehicle
Add registration number, capacity, and acquisition cost. Status starts as Available.
2
Onboard a driver
License, category, and expiry are validated before the driver can be dispatched.
3
Dispatch a trip
Cargo weight is checked against capacity, then vehicle and driver flip to On Trip.
4
Track & report
Completion frees both assets and rolls fuel, cost, and ROI into your reports.
